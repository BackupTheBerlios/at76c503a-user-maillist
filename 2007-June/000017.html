2b. There seems to be a slight impression that I've been into "problem reports" here (in the sense of "bug" submissions).
FWIW, to clarify my intentions I'll make a few comments:
If one looks closely, all my thread turns out to have dealt with mismanagement, not problems/bugs.
Bugs can happen in the best families, and we all know Linux programmers/developers are among the best, most enthusiastic and generous.
I don't see a problem here.
The real _problem_ has always been "mismanagement", as they say in corporate circles.
A few specific examples from this thread (which should be clearly taken as _constructive_ criticisms and presented with a smile on my face):

2b1.  I started the thread after an eternity, so to speak, of banging my head against the compile-averse v13 driver.
Joerg could've spared a lot of inconvenience to _himself_ first and to others by adding a simple line on his download site to the effect that the victim
should join a user-list first and _then_, maybe, write to his prominently displayed address directly (and only with personal matters, I suppose - He never answered my original mail on the v13 compile issue).
It might also help a little mention that there just might be some other folks hanging around updating and keeping his excellent original work in sync with all the kernel iterations, etc.

2b2.  Your reply (super helpful, as I said repeatedly) was presented in a caj-like manner (hey, didn't you know there's a driver around, to get you out
of your misery?) instead of being properly published somewhere to avoid somebody else falling into the same trap.

2b3.  The approach above also wrongly conditions the victim to expect that - if she knows anything about computers at all - on "honest" failure to expect another fix floating around and not to blame herself unnecessarily and/or foolishly press to the bitter end.
This is why this time I stopped my futile attempts to set up the driver while a "Message" like that was hovering above my head and asked for help as soon as I could ("help", in the sense, hey, where's the fix? Not what I did wrong or missed).
